# Player and controls guide

## Premise and campaign

Your colony ship has crashed on an alien world being processed by an ancient
machine. Recover suit modules, reconnect the regional transit network, repair
the shipwreck hub, and sever the Architect from the planetary Core.

The campaign is nonlinear. The map shows discovered regions, gates, current
position, and route requirements. Connections become bidirectional after they
are traversable. Every region has eight named rooms, a landmark, two optional
secret-room discoveries, two lore records, a save station, a shortcut, a
regional traversal sequence, and a combat or narrative climax.

## Default actions

The game displays live binding strings in menus and tutorials. Defaults are:

| Action | Keyboard | Gamepad intent |
|---|---|---|
| Move / navigate | Arrow keys or WASD | Left stick / D-pad |
| Jump / confirm | Space / Enter | South face button |
| Shoot / charge | Z / bound Shoot action | Right bumper (plus preset binding) |
| Dash | X | West face button |
| Grapple | C | Left bumper or Back/View |
| Interact | E | North face button |
| Map | M | D-pad up |
| Pause / back | Escape | Start/Back binding |
| Fullscreen | F11 | Bound Fullscreen action |

Use the Controls screen as the authority if a preset or platform changes a
default. Menu prompts never intentionally hard-code a conflicting key.

## Ability order

1. Wall Jump and Double Jump are installed in every starting suit. Double Jump
   is never withheld from geometry authored around its reach.
2. Spore Mother grants Dash, opening the Fungal-to-Crystal route.
3. Crystal Wyrm grants Charge Shot; Underground Lake offers the same module as
   a nonlinear alternate acquisition path.
4. Magma Core grants Ground Pound.
5. Overgrowth grants Grapple.

Charge Shot has partial and full tiers. A full charge breaks two-durability
walls. Ground Pound breaks vulnerable floors and staggers nearby ordinary
enemies. Grapple requires a visible anchor within range and preserves bounded
momentum when released.

## Death, checkpoints, and salvage

Campaign play has no lives counter. Death returns you to the latest save
station in that region, or the region entrance. Permanent progression stays.
Standard and Veteran place a recoverable portion of unbanked salvage at one
death marker; a later death replaces the old marker. Explorer loses no salvage.
The pause menu's **Return to Checkpoint** command is a no-penalty escape from a
stuck room or missed jump; it does not count as a death or drop salvage.

Contextual tutorial cards expire after seven seconds and can be dismissed with
Back without acknowledging them. The Controls screen can replay guidance, so a
tutorial never has to remain over gameplay merely to stay discoverable.

## Hub and postgame

Major bosses return you to the evolving shipwreck. Spend banked salvage at the
Workbench, inspect discoveries in Archive, choose routes on Map, and launch
postgame modes from Simulator. Time Trials unlock per completed region. Boss
Rush and New Game+ unlock after The Architect. Starting New Game+ requires a
second confirmation and retains abilities, upgrades, max HP, salvage, lore,
bestiary, keycards, shrine records, transit discovery, and room maps.
